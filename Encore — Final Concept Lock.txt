The Full Loop
You face an unkillable boss. Each loop, the boss reveals a new attack pattern — first loop it's just basic strikes, second loop it adds a sweep, third a charge-up nuke, etc. Your damage dealt in a loop converts to XP at the end of the loop, which permanently buffs your character's stats (and your ghosts inherit the buffs from the loop they were recorded in — or we keep it simple and only the live player levels up).
This solves the core game design problem beautifully: the boss escalates, but so do you AND your ghost army. The tension is whether your growth outpaces the boss's new tricks.
Why This Is Actually Great Now
The narrative writes itself: you're a hero who keeps dying to a god-tier boss, and each death you come back stronger with another ghost beside you. The final loop is a crescendo — five translucent heroes plus live-you all attacking in perfect choreography against a boss unleashing its ultimate move. That's a screenshot. That's the demo moment.
Boss Move Progression (5 Loops to Victory)
Loop 1 — boss only uses Basic Strike (telegraphed, easy to guard). You'll do minimal damage, die, get baseline XP. Loop 2 — adds Sweep (hits through guard if mistimed). Now you have one ghost, ~2x damage output. Loop 3 — adds Charge Beam (big windup, huge damage if not blocked perfectly). Two ghosts. Loop 4 — adds Phase Shift (boss briefly invulnerable, punishes mashing). Three ghosts. Loop 5 — adds Desperation (boss attacks every beat). Four ghosts + leveled-up live-you = victory window.
Tune numbers in the last 20 minutes. Don't pre-balance — playtest and adjust.
Leveling (Keep It Tiny)
Three stats only: ATK, DEF, HP. Damage dealt in a loop = XP. Every 100 XP = +1 to a stat the player picks between loops on a quick screen ("Level Up! Choose: +ATK / +DEF / +HP"). Single keypress, no menus. Ghosts use the stats they had when recorded — this makes early ghosts weaker but still useful as "chip damage," while late ghosts are heavy hitters. Emergent strategy without you designing it.
What This Doesn't Change
Timeline, tech stack, team split, deployment plan — all the same. The boss-pattern escalation is just a switch(loopNumber) in the boss AI. Leveling is three integer variables and one between-loop screen. Maybe 30 extra minutes of dev work, slotted into the polish window.
One Risk to Flag
Don't let the boss patterns get too complex to read. Each new attack needs a clear telegraph — a color flash, a sound cue, a wind-up animation. If players can't tell what's coming, the timing mechanic becomes frustrating instead of rewarding. Spend real time on telegraphs in the polish phase. This is the difference between "wow" and "huh?"
